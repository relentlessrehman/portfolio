Every year, thousands of students across Pakistan relocate to new cities for
university. Along with the excitement of a new campus and a new chapter comes
one of the most stressful and unstructured parts of student life: finding a
place to live. For most students, the process still relies on word of mouth,
unreliable Facebook groups, and property listings that are outdated,
unverified, or simply misleading. There is no dedicated platform built around
the specific needs of Pakistani students. Stayza exists to change that.

## The problem

Student housing in Pakistan operates almost entirely outside formal systems.
Landlords list properties informally, students negotiate blind, and there is
little transparency around pricing, safety, or proximity to campus.
International platforms do not account for local context, and general
property listing sites are not designed with a student's priorities in mind:
affordability, distance from university, roommate compatibility, and trust.

This gap is not a small inconvenience. It shapes where students choose to
study, how much stress they carry into their first semester, and how
vulnerable they are to scams or unsafe living conditions. Solving it requires
more than a listings website. It requires a platform built specifically
around the student experience.

## Building Stayza as a solo founder

Stayza started as an idea I could not stop thinking about while studying
Computer Science at SEECS, NUST. Rather than treating it as a side project, I
approached it as a real product from day one: identifying the core user
problem, designing a platform architecture that could scale, and building the
technical foundation myself.

Working solo meant owning every layer of the product. I designed and
implemented the frontend using React and TypeScript, building out more than
sixteen components to support search, filtering, and listing discovery in a
way that feels intuitive rather than overwhelming. On the backend, I used
Supabase to manage authentication and data, which meant solving real
engineering challenges around Row Level Security policies, OAuth integration,
and role based access control to keep user data secure while supporting
multiple account types.

One of the more demanding parts of the build was the analytics layer. A
platform like Stayza needs to understand user behavior, listing performance,
and search patterns to improve over time, but that data has to be handled
carefully. I built a secure analytics engine with RLS policies designed from
the ground up to protect user privacy while still surfacing the insights
needed to guide product decisions.

## Balancing building and learning

Building Stayza alongside a full course load has meant learning to work in
tight, focused sprints. Concepts from coursework in object oriented
programming, database systems, and software design have directly shaped
decisions in the product, from how the data model is structured to how
components are organized for maintainability. At the same time, the demands
of running a startup, debugging authentication flows late at night, refining
UI based on user feedback, and planning feature roadmaps have taught lessons
no classroom could replicate.

## Growing beyond a solo effort

As Stayza matured, it became clear that building a sustainable platform meant
building a team, not just a product. I recently developed a full set of
onboarding and operational documentation to support a growing intern program
across business development and quality assurance. Structuring this program
required thinking carefully about communication, accountability, and how to
maintain quality while delegating responsibility, skills that are just as
important as writing code.

This phase also meant looking outward. I studied existing platforms in the
space to understand where Stayza could differentiate itself, and began
preparing applications for competitions like the Punjab Youth Innovation and
Incubation Competition, seeking both validation and resources to accelerate
growth.

## What comes next

Stayza is still early, but the direction is clear. The goal is not just to
list properties, but to build trust into every part of the student housing
search: verified listings, transparent pricing, and a platform that treats
student housing as the significant life decision it actually is.

Building a startup as a first year student comes with real constraints on
time, resources, and experience. But it has also forced a kind of discipline
and resourcefulness that I believe will shape how I approach every project
going forward, in Stayza and beyond.

Stayza is a reminder that meaningful problems do not wait for the right time
to be solved. Sometimes the best way to understand a problem deeply enough to
fix it is to have lived through it yourself.
